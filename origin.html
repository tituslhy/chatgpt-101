The Unlikely Parents

OpenAI was founded in December 2015 by what can only be described as an Avengers team assembled by a casting director who'd had too much coffee: Sam Altman (YC president), Elon Musk, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, Reid Hoffman, Peter Thiel, and others. The founding premise was genuinely noble — and also completely unhinged. Their safety plan was to build the dangerous thing first. It's like saying "nuclear weapons are extremely dangerous, so we're going to build one in our garage and publish the blueprints so nobody has an unfair advantage." The logic is internally consistent. The vibe is absolutely unhinged.

Sam Altman — The Weather Event

Sam Altman is not a person. Sam Altman is a weather event that happens to wear a hoodie and speak in complete sentences about superintelligence. He co-founded OpenAI with no equity and no salary. Then in 2024, he received approximately 7% equity — worth roughly $11 billion. The "I didn't do this for money" arc ending with $11 billion is a narrative pivot of impressive proportions.

💡 Three-Word Sam Altman Summary

Visionary. Contradictory. Inevitable. He believes AI is potentially the most dangerous technology ever built AND builds it faster than almost anyone. He founded a nonprofit to prevent any one entity from controlling AI AND that entity is now a for-profit company where he holds $11B in equity. He's not a hypocrite exactly — he's resolved genuine dilemmas in ways that happen to be very convenient for his company's valuation. Both things are true simultaneously. This is called being Sam Altman.

The Worldcoin Interlude. While building OpenAI, Altman co-founded Worldcoin — a company that deploys silver orb-shaped devices in shopping malls, scans your iris in exchange for cryptocurrency, and operates under regulatory investigation in multiple countries. Spain banned it. Kenya suspended it. The Orbs kept scanning eyeballs in Southeast Asian malls. Ten million irises scanned for crypto. We mention this because you deserve the full picture of the man building the most powerful AI on earth.

The Boardroom Coup — 96 Hours That Broke The Internet

November 17, 2023. A Friday. 3pm Pacific. The OpenAI board fires Sam Altman. In an email. With four hours' notice.

Friday 3pm
Fired by email. 4 hours notice.
Goes to the building. Not allowed in. Posts a visitor badge selfie while being evicted. Ice in his veins.
Friday evening
Greg Brockman resigns in solidarity
President and co-founder gone within hours. Board starts interviewing replacement CEOs.
Saturday
Microsoft announces Altman is joining their AI lab
Microsoft stock goes UP. OpenAI implied valuation goes DOWN. The board accidentally made their biggest investor very happy. Genius. Truly.
Saturday
95% of ~770 employees sign open letter
"We will quit and follow Altman to Microsoft unless he's reinstated." That is not a revolt. That is a referendum.
Sunday
Ilya Sutskever tweets he "deeply regrets" his role
While. Still. On. The. Board. The man voted to fire Altman and publicly announced regret before the smoke cleared. Stunning behaviour.
Monday
Altman reinstated. Board members gone.
Walks back in without a visitor badge. Takes no public victory lap. Says he has "a lot of love" for the people who fired him. Terrifying composure.

The board tried to fire the main character of their own show. The entire cast refused to film without him. The network (Microsoft) called and said 'we'll just move the show to our channel.' The board blinked. What this revealed: Altman and the mission had become so intertwined that removing one broke the other.

The For-Profit Conversion

In late 2024, OpenAI completed its conversion to a fully for-profit benefit corporation. Valuation hits $157B at conversion, climbing toward $850B by mid-2026. The "open" in OpenAI hasn't been accurate since 2019 when they stopped publishing model weights. The "nonprofit" structure is now a minority stakeholder in an $850B company. The name is historical fiction — and Elon Musk has filed multiple lawsuits over exactly this point.

💡 The PayPal Mafia Moment of AI

PayPal spawned LinkedIn, YouTube, Yelp, Tesla, SpaceX. OpenAI spawned the entire modern AI industry. One company. One building in San Francisco. The alumni didn't wait to graduate — they left mid-semester, raised billions, and started competing with the school. OpenAI trained the researchers who then competed with it. This is the most expensive talent development program in history.
