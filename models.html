The "o" Series — The Thinkers

September 2024: what if we made the model think before it speaks? Reasoning models generate internal "thinking tokens" — a scratchpad where the model works through the problem before the final answer. The student who reads the question twice before writing.

📊 Why o2 Doesn't Exist

Samsung was launching a phone with "O2" branding in a partnership. OpenAI skipped from o1 to o3 to avoid the trademark collision. The most powerful AI company on earth named their model series around a phone release schedule. We don't deserve this timeline.

DeepSeek — Wall Street's Very Bad Monday

January 20, 2025. DeepSeek — a Chinese AI lab funded by a hedge fund — releases DeepSeek R1: a reasoning model competitive with OpenAI's o1, open source, reportedly trained for $6 million. OpenAI spent $100M+ on GPT-4. DeepSeek's response to prevailing wisdom: "Lol."

🎨 DALL-E / ChatGPT Images 2.0

ChatGPT Images 2.0 is #1 on Image Arena — beating Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, and Gemini's image models. Nothing at S$27/month touches it for image generation. Now renders text inside images without producing ransom note vibes. This is ChatGPT's most unambiguous advantage over Claude, which has zero native image generation.

🎤 GPT-4o Realtime / Advanced Voice

May 2024. THE demo. The AI was mid-sentence — the human interrupted it. Mid-word. And the AI adapted in real time without missing a beat. No restart. Just pivoted. Then it sang. Then got flirtatious enough that OpenAI walked it back because people were getting emotionally attached in ways that raised eyebrows. Why it was hard: previous voice AI was Speech-to-Text → LLM → Text-to-Speech, three latency hits. GPT-4o handled audio natively end-to-end. Claude's voice uses a smart WebSocket pipeline — Titus interrupted it in his car, it adapted. Different architecture. Surprisingly close result.

🖼️ Canvas

October 2024. Document lives in a panel, changes happen IN the document, chat on the side. Revolutionary concept that somehow took two years to exist.

💡 The Irony

Anthropic shipped Artifacts in June 2024 — four months before Canvas. Canvas was OpenAI seeing what Anthropic built and shipping their own version with more collaborative editing polish. Titus's favourite ChatGPT feature exists because Anthropic shipped it first. Take that.

💡 The Most Backwards Hierarchy In Software

The company that owns the browser has the least control. The company with a plugin has medium control. The company with a phone app has maximum control — including over the browser the first company owns. Owning the intelligence layer beats owning the distribution layer. Probably.
