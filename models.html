The "o" Series — The Thinkers

September 2024: what if we made the model think before it speaks? Reasoning models generate internal "thinking tokens" — a scratchpad where the model works through the problem before the final answer. The student who reads the question twice before writing.

📊 Why o2 Doesn't Exist

Samsung was launching a phone with "O2" branding in a partnership. OpenAI skipped from o1 to o3 to avoid the trademark collision. The most powerful AI company on earth named their model series around a phone release schedule. We don't deserve this timeline.

DeepSeek — Wall Street's Very Bad Monday

January 20, 2025. DeepSeek — a Chinese AI lab funded by a hedge fund — releases DeepSeek R1: a reasoning model competitive with OpenAI's o1, open source, reportedly trained for $6 million. OpenAI spent $100M+ on GPT-4. DeepSeek's response to prevailing wisdom: "Lol."

The Product EcosystemFrom Chatbox To Armory

ChatGPT and Claude are not Swiss Army knives useful for some situations. They're Swiss Army ARMORIES. Heavy firepower for when you need to bring the wall down. And at $30/month it's unbelievable value. Genuinely.

— Titus Lim · 2026

🎨 DALL-E / ChatGPT Images 2.0

ChatGPT Images 2.0 is #1 on Image Arena — beating Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, and Gemini's image models. Nothing at S$27/month touches it for image generation. Now renders text inside images without producing ransom note vibes. This is ChatGPT's most unambiguous advantage over Claude, which has zero native image generation.

🎤 GPT-4o Realtime / Advanced Voice

May 2024. THE demo. The AI was mid-sentence — the human interrupted it. Mid-word. And the AI adapted in real time without missing a beat. No restart. Just pivoted. Then it sang. Then got flirtatious enough that OpenAI walked it back because people were getting emotionally attached in ways that raised eyebrows. Why it was hard: previous voice AI was Speech-to-Text → LLM → Text-to-Speech, three latency hits. GPT-4o handled audio natively end-to-end. Claude's voice uses a smart WebSocket pipeline — Titus interrupted it in his car, it adapted. Different architecture. Surprisingly close result.

🖼️ Canvas

October 2024. Document lives in a panel, changes happen IN the document, chat on the side. Revolutionary concept that somehow took two years to exist.

💡 The Irony

Anthropic shipped Artifacts in June 2024 — four months before Canvas. Canvas was OpenAI seeing what Anthropic built and shipping their own version with more collaborative editing polish. Titus's favourite ChatGPT feature exists because Anthropic shipped it first. Take that.

🤖 Custom GPTs — The App Store That Became A Gift Shop

🔶 Analogy

Finetuning = training a student from scratch on your textbooks. Custom GPT = giving the same GPT-4o a detailed briefing sheet before every exam. GPT-4o in a costume. The App Store vision never materialised. What worked: building your OWN Custom GPT for private use.

⚙️ The FastAPI Dumping Ground

The Codex product was built in seven weeks while one engineer woke to a newborn at 5:30am. A dumping ground powering one billion users. 💡 The Most Backwards Hierarchy In Software

The company that owns the browser has the least control. The company with a plugin has medium control. The company with a phone app has maximum control — including over the browser the first company owns. Owning the intelligence layer beats owning the distribution layer. Probably.

How To Use ChatGPTThe Actual Operating Manual

The Rule

ChatGPT is not one product anymore. It is a chat app, research assistant, image studio, voice interface, spreadsheet analyst, custom-tool launcher, coding agent, browser, and app connector layer wearing the same logo. The mistake is asking "Can ChatGPT do this?" The better question is: which surface should I use?

The Surfaces

SurfaceUse it forDo not use it for
ChatGPT appDaily thinking, writing, tutoring, ideation, summaries, prompts, analysis, images, voice, projects, GPTs, files, and connected apps.Large codebase changes where the model needs repo access, terminal output, tests, and file edits.
ProjectsLong-running work with context: a course, client, research theme, trip, business plan, writing project, or personal operating system.One-off questions. Do not turn every random chat into a project.
Custom GPTsRepeatable workflows: your investing analyst, lesson planner, writing editor, prompt coach, sales-call prep bot, or internal SOP assistant.Things that need fresh judgment every time. A GPT is a reusable brief, not a magic employee.
GPTs by othersQuick access to niche tools, community workflows, tutoring formats, and specialized assistants you do not want to configure yourself.Anything private, sensitive, or business-critical unless you understand what data and actions the GPT uses.
Connected appsPulling context from Google Drive, SharePoint, GitHub, Notion, Linear, Dropbox, Box, Teams, Outlook, and other work systems without copy-paste.Blindly granting access to everything. Connect the minimum set of sources needed for the job.
Codex app / cloudAsync coding: implement features, fix bugs, explain codebases, run tests, prepare PRs, and hand back diffs for review.Micro-edits where opening the repo yourself is faster.
Codex CLITerminal-first repo work: local edits, test loops, debugging, refactors, scripts, and disciplined engineering tasks.Vague product thinking. Give it a concrete task and a repo.
Codex IDE/editorInteractive coding while you stay close to the files: pair-programming, review, focused changes, and context-aware edits.Fire-and-forget multi-hour work. Use cloud/app for that.
AtlasWeb work with context: summarize pages, compare products, research across tabs, remember browsing context, and let agent mode act in the browser.Sensitive transactions without supervision. Browser agents can click the wrong thing with confidence.
Mobile / desktop appFast capture: voice notes, screenshots, photos, files, dictation, quick questions, and turning messy context into structured output.Deep multi-file coding or huge research jobs when a larger screen and saved project context matter.

Compared With Claude Design

Claude Design is the Figma-like frontend prototyping lane: build interactive UI mockups fast, inspect the feel, iterate visually. ChatGPT's equivalent stack is split: Images for visual assets, Canvas for docs/code, Codex for real implementation, and Atlas for web-context work. More surfaces. More power.
